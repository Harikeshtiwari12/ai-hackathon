00 — The Why

Let's be honest for a second.

Most hackathons end with cool demos that never see production. Slides get clapped at. Prototypes get forgotten. Everyone goes back to doing things the old way.

Not this one.

This hackathon has one filter: Would you actually use this next week?

We're not looking for the fanciest AI model or the most buzzword-heavy pitch. We want the tool that makes Prem's Monday easier. The bot that saves Suneet's team 3 hours of repetitive work. The system that helps Adarsh's reps know exactly who to call next.

If it doesn't solve a real problem for a real person at TranZact, it doesn't count.

09 — Real Talk

Advice from the people who've seen hackathons fail.

Start with the person, not the technology. Don't ask "what can AI do?" Ask "what's the most annoying part of Vivian's day?" or "why does Himanshu Jain's team lose deals?" The best AI tools are invisible — they just make the pain go away.

Prototype ugly. Ship fast. Nobody cares if your UI looks like it was designed in 2004 — if it solves a real problem, it wins. A Google Sheet connected to an API is a valid prototype. A WhatsApp bot that actually works beats a beautiful dashboard that doesn't.

Don't force AI where it doesn't belong. If the best solution is a simple automation without any AI, that's fine. We're scoring business impact, not how many times you say "machine learning" in your demo.

Talk to your users during the build. If you're building for CX, go sit with the CX team for 30 minutes. Watch them work. Ask what's broken. The insight you get from watching > the insight you get from assuming.

Don't build the smartest AI.
